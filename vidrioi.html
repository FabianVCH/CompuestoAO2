Glass : We will show you what it is and how to recycle it.

Glass is an inorganic material hard , brittle , transparent amorphous found in nature but can also be produced by man. Artificial glass used to make windows , glasses, bottles and a variety of products.

Glass recycling

Glass is an ideal material to be recycled , can be recycled to 100 % , an infinite number of times. The use of recycled glass helps save energy , is less expensive , pluss to reduce waste and the consumption of raw materials. You glass can be used to make alguas dinnerware, also is useful in multiple processes and materials such as bricks , ceramics , asphalt , etc., saving raw materials and reducing the amount of waste sent to vertedero.1 The recycled glass requires 26 % less energy than their manufacture from scratch and reduces by 20 % the emissions to the atmosphere from the manufacture , contaminating 40% less water than the manufacture of glass from sand , lime and soda . Each tonne of waste that is recycled glass prevents 315 pounds of carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere during the manufacture of glass

Procedure

Glass is an important and abundant component in industrial waste and domestic , local landfills present usually consists of glass bottles, glassware, bulbs and other objects , moreover, in the manufacture of glass occurs high rate of defective production should be recycled . For all this , waste glass are abundant , leading many countries to establish methods of collection of glass . At times the various types of glass are chemically incompatible , so that a selection is required prior to processing. For example, the heat-resistant glass such as Pyrex or borosilicate glass should not be placed in the container glass recycling , since only a piece of the material viscosity would alter the properties of the fluid in the furnace at the time of re- melt the mixture . Due to incompatibilities , recycling channels are set apart , even to glass separated according to colors . Once collected, the first step in the recycling of glass, is cleaning the glass is treated with chemicals to remove any dirt , sand or grease are removed below plastic items , paper and other waste. Once clean , the glass is passed through a series of screens and hammers, to achieve the desired granulometry. Below is passed by some magnets that remove possible traces of metal , glass and ready to be crushed again melted called cullet . The cullet is heated to 1600 degrees , mixed with 50% sand, limestone and sodium hydroxide to produce new products that will have identical properties with respect to glass manufactured directly from natural resources.
